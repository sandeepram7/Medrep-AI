## Key Clinical Summary for Your Agent

**Critical Differences for Prescribing:**

1. **DAPT Decision** : Use Aspirin+Clopidogrel for 12 months post-PCI/ACS; then clopidogrel alone may be preferred over aspirin for long-term maintenance in high-risk patients.[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih**+1**](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11358762/)
2. **PPI with Clopidogrel** : Always choose pantoprazole (or rabeprazole) over omeprazole/esomeprazole when the patient is on clopidogrel to avoid loss of antiplatelet effect.[pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih**+1**](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21464720/)
3. **NSAID + Aspirin** : Avoid ibuprofen in patients on low-dose aspirin for cardioprotection; use diclofenac or paracetamol instead.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2737651/)]
4. **Metformin in Heart Failure** : Historically contraindicated but now considered safe in stable HF with eGFR >30; preferred over sulfonylureas in diabetic heart patients.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5902828/)]
5. **SABA Overuse Warning** : Patients using salbutamol >3 times/week have poorly controlled asthma and need stepped-up ICS therapy.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10137101/)]
